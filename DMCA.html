Heir Tight™ DMCA Notice

Effective Date: 01/04/2021
Last Updated: 10/01/2025

Heir Tight, LLC (“Heir Tight,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) respects the intellectual property rights of others and requires our users to do the same. Pursuant to the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. §512) (“DMCA”), Heir Tight will respond promptly to valid claims of copyright infringement on our platform.

Heir Tight provides users with a private, encrypted environment to store and share documents. While most uploads are personal, family, or business files, some users may upload content that includes copyrighted material. This policy explains how we handle any such claims.

1. Submitting a DMCA Takedown Notice
If you believe material stored or shared through Heir Tight infringes your copyright, you may send a written notice (“DMCA Notice”) to our designated Copyright Agent including the following:

• Your physical or electronic signature.
• Identification of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed.
• Identification of the material you believe is infringing (including the specific folder name, file name, or account identifier, if known) and information reasonably sufficient for Heir Tight to locate it.
• Your contact information (name, address, telephone number, and email).
• A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
• A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information provided is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

Please send your notice to:

DMCA Agent — Heir Tight, LLC
Email: legal@getheirtight.com
Mailing Address:

2. How We Process DMCA Notices
Upon receiving a valid DMCA Notice:
• We will promptly review and verify the claim.
• If the material is found to be infringing or likely infringing, it will be disabled or removed.
• We will notify the user who uploaded the material and provide them with an opportunity to submit a Counter-Notification.

Because Heir Tight is a private document vault, access to the material is generally limited to the user and their designated heirs; however, we treat all valid DMCA claims seriously and act in good faith to resolve them.

3. Submitting a Counter-Notification
If your material was removed in response to a DMCA Notice and you believe this was in error or that you are authorized to use the material, you may submit a Counter-Notification that includes:

• Your physical or electronic signature.
• Identification of the removed material and its previous location on the platform.
• A statement that you have a good faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
• Your name, address, telephone number, and email address.
• A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for your judicial district, or if outside the U.S., to the jurisdiction of the Northern District of Georgia, and that you will accept service of process from the person who submitted the DMCA Notice.

Counter-Notifications should be sent to legal@getheirtight.com.

4. Repeat Infringers
In accordance with the DMCA and other applicable laws, Heir Tight has adopted a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances and at our sole discretion, the accounts of users who are deemed to be repeat infringers.

5. No Legal Advice or Mediation
Heir Tight acts as a neutral service provider. We are not in a position to determine whether the content at issue infringes a copyright. Our role is limited to facilitating the DMCA process in good faith.

6. Misrepresentations
Under Section 512(f) of the DMCA, any person who knowingly misrepresents that material or activity is infringing may be subject to liability for damages, including costs and attorneys’ fees. Please ensure your claim or counter-claim is accurate and made in good faith.
